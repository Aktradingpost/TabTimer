## Information Stored Locally on Your Device

TabTimer stores the following data **locally on your device only** using Chrome's built-in storage API (`chrome.storage.local`):

| Data Type | Purpose | Shared? |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Scheduled URLs | To open websites at your specified times | No |
| Schedule settings | Time, frequency, and repeat options you configure | No |
| Extension preferences | Your chosen settings (theme, display options) | No |
| License information | To verify premium feature access | No |
| Backup data | To allow you to restore your schedules | No |

**This data never leaves your device** and is not transmitted to any external servers.

## Chrome Permissions Explained

TabTimer requests the following permissions to function:

### `storage`
- **Why:** To save your scheduled URLs and settings locally on your device
- **Data Access:** Only TabTimer's own saved data; cannot access other extensions' data

### `tabs`
- **Why:** To open new tabs at your scheduled times
- **Data Access:** Only used to create new tabs; we do not read or track your browsing history or existing tabs

### `alarms`
- **Why:** To trigger scheduled events at the correct times
- **Data Access:** Only creates timing events; no user data involved

### `contextMenus`
- **Why:** To allow right-click menu options for quickly adding the current page to schedules
- **Data Access:** Only the URL you explicitly choose to schedule

### `host_permissions` (all URLs)
- **Why:** To open any website you schedule at the designated time
- **Data Access:** Only opens URLs you have explicitly scheduled; we do not access, read, or modify webpage content
