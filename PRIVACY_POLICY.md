LEGAL Privacy Policy
Effective date: March 31, 2026  ·  TabTimer Pro
Overview
TabTimer Pro ("TabTimer", "we", "our") is a browser extension for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge that schedules websites to open automatically. This policy explains what data we handle, how we handle it, and your rights.
The short version: we collect nothing, and all your data stays on your device.
Data We Do Not Collect
TabTimer does not collect, transmit, store, or share any of the following:
	•	Personal identifiers (name, email address, phone number, etc.)
	•	Browsing history or the URLs you schedule
	•	Usage analytics or telemetry
	•	Device information or IP addresses
	•	Cookies or tracking identifiers of any kind
Local Data Storage
All schedule data you create in TabTimer (URLs, times, repeat settings, etc.) is stored locally on your device using the browser's built-in chrome.storage / browser.storage API. This data never leaves your browser.
If you use the optional Google Drive backup feature (Chrome only), your schedule data is saved to your own Google Drive account. TabTimer accesses only the files it creates — it does not read any other Drive content.
🔑 Google API — Limited Use Disclosure
Google API Services — Limited Use Policy
TabTimer Pro's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.
Specifically:
	•	TabTimer only requests access to Google Drive in order to save and restore your schedule backup file.
	•	We do not use Google user data for any purpose other than this backup and restore functionality.
	•	We do not transfer Google user data to third parties.
	•	We do not use Google user data for advertising or to train machine learning models.
	•	We do not allow humans to read your Google user data unless required by law or you have given us explicit permission.
Third-Party Services
TabTimer does not integrate with any analytics, advertising, or data-broker services. The extension makes no external network requests except for the optional Google Drive backup feature described above.
Children's Privacy
TabTimer is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect any data from children.
Changes to This Policy
If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the effective date above and post the updated policy at this URL. Continued use of TabTimer after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
Contact
Questions about this privacy policy? Open an issue on our GitHub repository or contact us via the Chrome Web Store listing.
